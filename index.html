Welcome to SFES

SFES (Sustainable Farming with E-Soil) is our innovative approach toward a greener future. We combine modern hydroponics with an electrically-enhanced substrate called E-Soil, designed to boost plant growth by nearly 70%. Our goal is to revolutionize sustainable agriculture using clean technology and accessible materials.

Our Sustainable Farming Model

Our model integrates hydroponics with a specially engineered substrate—E-Soil. This combination enables faster plant growth, higher nutrient absorption, and climate-friendly farming. The system is designed to be low-maintenance, eco-friendly, and efficient, making it suitable for both indoor setups and large-scale farming. The electricity voltage increases the nitrogen solubility of the soil increasing plant growth , morevor with the use of cellulose in form of cocopeat we stack the stats for increased plant growth.

What is E-Soil?

E-Soil is a voltage-activated substrate created using coco peat, a cellulose-rich material known for high water retention and superior aeration. Cellulose naturally conducts electricity better than typical soil. When we introduce a controlled electric voltage, nitrogen solubility increases significantly, enhancing nutrient uptake and accelerating physical growth of the plant.

This makes E-Soil a powerful upgrade to traditional hydroponic media.

Our Experiments

We conducted controlled experiments on barley seeds using regular coco peat versus E-Soil. Plants grown in E-Soil showed:

  • Faster germination
  • Stronger and longer root development
  • Nearly 70% higher physical growth
  • Healthier leaf structure
  • Lesser wastage of water and far more production

These results demonstrate the effectiveness of voltage-enhanced substrates in promoting plant health.

Plants Used in the Model

For the live model demonstration, we chose the Spider Plant because:

  • It is an excellent indoor air-purifying plant
  • Requires minimal maintenance
  • Responds well to hydroponic environments
  • Shows visible growth improvements in E-Soil

Earlier tests were performed on barley, which displayed remarkable growth enhancement.
